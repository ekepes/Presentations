So You Want to be a Technical Leader

Eric Kepes

Pittsburgh TechFest
June 13, 2015

Why do you want to be a technical leader?

Bad reasons

Money, fame, glory
Being “in charge"

Good reasons

Share your experience
Help other people become leaders

How?

Do's

Trust your team
Work with your team
Take blame, even when it’s not your fault. Share credit, in fact, give away more than you should.
Stay relevant/Stay technical
Say, “I don’t know, let’s find out"
Have values you adhere to

Don'ts

Don't do everything yourself (learn to delegate). Let the team decide things.
Don't prevent your team from making mistakes (mostly)
Don't lead witch hunt code reviews
Don't be dogmatic about things that aren’t important

So,

Do You Have to be a Manager?

You can have an positive impact on your team and a good career as a technical leader. Even if you never get the title.

Manager != Leader

A Leader is someone people want to follow. Your team will choose you - can’t really force it. To help things along - be the kind of leader you’d want to work with
